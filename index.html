Kyiv

country: Ukraine

“Motherland” - a monumental sculpture on the right bank of the Dnieper - is one of the five tallest in the world, its height with a pedestal is 102 meters. For comparison, the Statue of Liberty is 93 meters high.


also interesting


The Arsenalnaya metro station in Kyiv is the deepest in the world; its platform and the surface of the earth are separated by 105.5 meters. Townspeople believe that secret passages connect the station with the Arsenal plant and a secret bunker for the country's leadership.

One of the symbols of Kyiv is the chestnut leaf, as these trees grow throughout the Ukrainian capital.

Warsaw

country: Poland

The Warsaw Palace of Culture is the tallest building in Poland, its height is 237 meters. The palace was given to the city by Stalin in Soviet times.


also interesting


also interesting In the center of the city stands the narrowest house in the world. Its width is only 122 centimeters and its height is 9 meters.

The real quest will be to find a circus in Warsaw - it simply isn’t here. But the number of theaters is much greater than cinemas.

Winnipooh is recognized as the most popular fairy-tale character in Warsaw. In 1954, Lenin Street was renamed in honor of him.

Warsaw is the leader among traffic jams. It is one of the most densely populated cities in Europe. Poles spend more than 100 hours in traffic jams per year. Here's another argument in favor of exciting walking tours.

Berlin

country: German

The tallest building in Germany is the television tower at Alexanderplatz. Its height is 368 meters.


also interesting


Berlin is the second largest and fifth largest city in the EU. In terms of population, the German capital is second only to London.

The first traffic light in Europe was installed in Berlin in 1924. A copy of it is still on Potsdamer Platz today.

Berlin is 9 times larger than Paris.

Berlin is famous for its museums, nightlife, universities and high standard of living. About 30% of its territory is occupied by parks (more than 2,500 parks), gardens and lakes (50 lakes and 3 rivers).

Prague

country: Czech

Prague Castle is the largest castle in the world.


also interesting


There are about a thousand towers and turrets in Prague, if you count municipal buildings, ancient fortresses, and private houses. Therefore, it enjoys the unofficial title of “city of towers”.

The age of the capital of the Czech Republic is about 1150 years. More precisely, it was founded in 880 AD.

Prague Castle is the largest castle in the world.

One of the most interesting places in Prague is the “Dancing House”, a complex of two towers, bizarrely curved against all the laws of physics according to the imagination of the architect. They symbolize a dancing couple.

Athens

country: Greece

The city once received its name in honor of the patron goddess Athena. According to legend, Athena and Poseidon competed for the right to patronize the city, but the inhabitants chose the goddess.


also interesting


also interesting In the center of the city stands the narrowest house in the world. Its width is only 122 centimeters and its height is 9 meters.

Despite its advanced age, Athens is the youngest European capital. The city received this status only in 1834.

This form of government as democracy arose here. And this happened about two and a half thousand years ago.

One of the most popular places among tourists visiting Greece is the Acropolis of Athens. This monumental structure is more than two thousand years old.
